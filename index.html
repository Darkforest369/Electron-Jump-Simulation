AMBIENT HEAT 0 K

Simulate a flame test environment by modifying thermal kinetic energy. Higher values excite electrons across broader state functions, establishing spontaneous photon decay fields.

Particle Mode: Shows electrons as classical point masses orbiting the nucleus.

Wave Mode: Shows electrons as de Broglie standing waves.

Visible Wavelength (nm):

Tune the laser to a specific visible photon wavelength (380-780nm). If the wavelength matches an exact quantum energy gap for the selected element, the lattice will absorb the photons and illuminate.

Zoom out far enough to abstract the quantum models into a physical lattice structure. Blocks glow during photon emission states. At extreme temperatures, the surface of the object succumbs to blackbody radiation and glows white-hot.

Toggling Alloy Mode reveals 8 specific alloys. Selecting one will mix the lattice grid. Manual keys (1-8) are disabled. You must use heat or lasers to extract the combined spectral fingerprint.

When fully zoomed out, the Laser module becomes available in Controls. Fire beams at the macroscopic structure to test quantum absorption gaps.

Hover your mouse over any glowing emission line on the live spectrometer panel to view precise analytical data, including the exact wavelength (nm) and the quantum decay transition path.

In Alloy Mode, the tooltip will also identify the specific element responsible for releasing that color.
